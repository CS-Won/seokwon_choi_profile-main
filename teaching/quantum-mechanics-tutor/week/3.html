Week 3 · Harmonic oscillator, free particle, scattering (Griffiths Ch. 2.3–2.5)

Spring 2025 · Yonsei PHY3101 — Tutor: Seokwon Choi

The quantum harmonic oscillator

The Hamiltonian is

H^=2mp^​2​+21​mω2x^2,

which comes up everywhere: small oscillations around any potential minimum, normal modes, the quantized EM field. We solve it the algebraic way using ladder operators, which generalizes directly to angular momentum and the Landau-level problem in the final.

Step 1 — Build the ladder operators

Define

a≡2ℏmω​​(x^+mωi​p^​),a†≡2ℏmω​​(x^−mωi​p^​).

From the canonical commutator [x^,p^​]=iℏ,

[a,a†]=2ℏmω​[x^+mωip^​​,x^−mωip^​​]=2ℏmω​⋅(−mω2i​[x^,p^​])=2ℏmω​⋅mω2ℏ​=1.

Next, express H^ in terms of a,a†. From the definitions,

x^=2mωℏ​​(a+a†),p^​=−i2mωℏ​​(a−a†).

Square them:

p^​2=−2mωℏ​(a−a†)2=2mωℏ​(−a2−(a†)2+aa†+a†a),
x^2=2mωℏ​(a2+(a†)2+aa†+a†a).

Plug into H^. The a2 and (a†)2 pieces cancel, leaving

H^=21​ℏω(aa†+a†a).

Use aa†=a†a+1 to get the canonical form

H^=ℏω(N^+21​),N^≡a†a.​

Step 2 — The ladder action

From [a,a†]=1 we get

[N^,a]=[a†a,a]=a†[a,a]+[a†,a]a=−a,[N^,a†]=a†.

If N^∣n⟩=n∣n⟩ then N^a†∣n⟩=a†N^∣n⟩+[N^,a†]∣n⟩=(n+1)a†∣n⟩ — i.e. a†∣n⟩∝∣n+1⟩. Similarly a∣n⟩∝∣n−1⟩. To fix the proportionality, compute ∥a†∣n⟩∥2:

⟨n∣aa†∣n⟩=⟨n∣(a†a+1)∣n⟩=n+1,

so a†∣n⟩=n+1​∣n+1⟩ (positive-phase convention) and similarly a∣n⟩=n​∣n−1⟩.

Eigenvalues of N^ are non-negative integers (any non-integer n would let a generate a state of negative norm), so we have a tower

En​=ℏω(n+21​),n=0,1,2,…​

Step 3 — Ground-state wave function

Apply aψ0​(x)=0. Writing a in position space,

2ℏmω​​(x+mωℏ​dxd​)ψ0​(x)=0,

which is the first-order ODE

ψ0′​(x)=−ℏmω​xψ0​(x)⟹ψ0​(x)=Cexp(−2ℏmω​x2).

Normalizing, C=(mω/(πℏ))1/4. The excited states follow:

ψn​(x)=n!​1​(a†)nψ0​(x).

Up to normalization, ψn​(x)∝Hn​(ξ)e−ξ2/2 with ξ=mω/ℏ​x and Hermite polynomials Hn​.

Free particle and Gaussian wave packets

With V=0 the stationary states are plane waves ϕk​(x)=eikx with energy ℏ2k2/(2m). These are not square integrable, but they form a generalized basis: any normalizable state is a Fourier integral

Ψ(x,t)=2π​1​∫−∞∞​ϕ(k)eikx−iω(k)tdk,ω(k)=2mℏk2​,

where the weight ϕ(k) is determined by the initial condition.

For a Gaussian initial state Ψ(x,0)∝e−x2/(4σ02​) the Fourier transform is also Gaussian, and a short calculation gives that the width grows as

σ(t)=σ0​1+(2mσ02​ℏt​)2​.

Wave packets spread, with the spreading rate larger for narrower initial packets — exactly the position–momentum uncertainty principle in motion.

Delta-function well

Take V(x)=−αδ(x) with α>0. Away from the origin the TISE reduces to ψ′′=κ2ψ with κ=−2mE​/ℏ, so normalizable bound states (E<0) look like

ψ(x)=Ae−κ∣x∣.

This ψ is continuous at x=0 but ψ′ has a kink. To capture the kink size, integrate the TISE from −ϵ to +ϵ and let ϵ→0:

−2mℏ2​[ψ′(0+)−ψ′(0−)]−αψ(0)=0,

i.e. the boundary condition

Δψ′(0)≡ψ′(0+)−ψ′(0−)=−ℏ22mα​ψ(0).

Plug in the symmetric ansatz ψ=Ae−κ∣x∣: Δψ′(0)=−2κA, so

−2κA=−ℏ22mα​A⟹κ=ℏ2mα​,E=−2ℏ2mα2​.

Exactly one bound state, no matter how small α.

Scattering off a step — full matching

V(x)=0 for x≤0 and V(x)=V0​ for x>0. Send a plane wave from the left:

ψL​(x)=Aeikx+Be−ikx,k=2mE​/ℏ.

Case E>V0​

Right region:

ψR​(x)=Feik′x,k′=2m(E−V0​)​/ℏ.

We drop a hypothetical incoming wave from the right (the problem sends particles from the left only). Match ψ,ψ′ at x=0:

A+B=F,ik(A−B)=ik′F.

Solve the linear system. From the second equation A−B=(k′/k)F; combined with A+B=F,

A=21​F(1+k′/k),B=21​F(1−k′/k),

so

AF​=k+k′2k​,AB​=k+k′k−k′​.

The reflection coefficient is the ratio of reflected to incident probability current,

R=∣A∣2∣B∣2​=(k+k′k−k′​)2.

Transmission and the k′/k subtlety

For the transmitted wave the speeds on the two sides differ, so a naive ∣F/A∣2 would be wrong. Compute the probability current j=(ℏ/m)Im(ψ∗ψ′) on each side. For a plane wave ψ=Ceiqx, j=(ℏq/m)∣C∣2, so

jinc​=mℏk​∣A∣2,jrefl​=mℏk​∣B∣2,jtr​=mℏk′​∣F∣2.

Therefore

T≡jinc​jtr​​=kk′​​AF​​2=kk′​⋅(k+k′)24k2​=(k+k′)24kk′​.

Sum:

R+T=(k+k′)2(k−k′)2+4kk′​=(k+k′)2(k+k′)2​=1.

Case E<V0​

Right region: ψR​(x)=Ce−κx with κ=2m(V0​−E)​/ℏ (drop the growing exponential). Matching:

A+B=C,ik(A−B)=−κC.

Solve:

AB​=ik−κik+κ​⟹R=​AB​​2=k2+κ2k2+κ2​=1.

Total reflection. The wave function still leaks into the wall over a length 1/κ, but no probability current crosses on average (jtr​=0 because ψR​ is real up to a global phase).

Mental model

At the end of Week 3 the 1D tool-kit is complete: infinite well (sine modes, energies ∝n2), finite well (transcendental, finite count), harmonic oscillator (evenly spaced ladder), delta well (one bound state), step/barrier scattering (R and T from matching, with the k′/k prefactor in T). The midterm in Week 4 uses every single one of these pieces.
